# KisanAI Pitch

## One-Line Pitch
KisanAI is a vernacular, source-aware AI farming assistant that helps Indian farmers make better daily crop decisions.

## 100-Word Pitch
India has 9.7 crore digitally traceable farmers, yet critical agricultural information — disease diagnosis, weather interpretation, mandi prices, government schemes — remains scattered, complex, and locked behind language barriers. KisanAI is a multilingual AI assistant that combines disease triage, weather-to-action advisory, mandi price context, and scheme eligibility into one simple conversation in the farmer's language. Unlike existing tools, every answer links to its source and discloses uncertainty. We are building the lowest-friction, highest-trust wrapper around existing agricultural information channels. Our wedge: one trusted assistant for recurring farming decisions, deployed through FPOs, KVKs, and NGOs.

## Government Pitch
**Problem:** Indian farmers face fragmented, complex, non-vernacular agricultural advisory. Information gaps cause preventable crop losses, delayed scheme adoption, and financial distress.

**Solution:** KisanAI is a multilingual AI assistant providing source-aware guidance for disease triage, weather interpretation, mandi prices, and government scheme eligibility — in the farmer's language.

**Public Impact:** Reduced information gap, faster access to schemes, better crop decisions, documented usage metrics.

**Deployment Model:** 90-day pilots through FPOs, KVKs, NGOs, and state agriculture departments.

**Safety:** Source-aware answers, uncertainty disclosure, expert escalation, no fake claims, DPDP Act compliant.

## Investor Pitch
**Vision:** Become the default AI layer for Indian agriculture — the lowest-friction, highest-trust interface between farmers and the information they need.

**Market:** 9.7 Cr digitally traceable farmers (PM-KISAN). TAM at Rs 600/farmer/year = Rs 58.2B. Rural internet at 41.7%. 5G in 99% districts.

**Why Now:** Bharat-VISTAAR validates government demand. FarmRise (5M users), DeHaat (Rs 3,000 Cr) validate category. RAG + LLMs make source-aware agriculture QA viable.

**Wedge:** Vernacular, messaging-first, source-aware advisory for disease triage, weather-to-action, mandi context, and scheme guidance. Not all agriculture — just recurring daily decisions.

**Business Model:** B2B2C (FPO/NGO partnerships) → Freemium → Enterprise API.

**Competition:** FarmRise (corporate trust deficit), DeHaat (commerce-first), Plantix (single-feature), Bharat-VISTAAR (government speed). We compete on trust and simplicity.

## Problem
Indian farmers lose money every season not because of lack of skill, but because critical information is scattered, late, or locked behind language and complexity barriers.

## Solution
One AI assistant that combines disease triage, weather advisory, mandi prices, and scheme guidance in the farmer's language — with source citations and uncertainty disclosure.

## Market
- TAM: 9.7 Cr farmers × Rs 600/year = Rs 58.2B
- SAM: 45% smartphone-enabled, internet-connected farmers
- SOM: 0.5% penetration by Year 3

## Why Now
- Rural internet: 41.7%
- 5G: 99% districts
- Bharat-VISTAAR launched 2026
- Category validated by FarmRise, DeHaat, Plantix

## Business Model
1. B2B2C: FPO/NGO partnerships (primary)
2. Freemium: Premium features for individual farmers
3. Enterprise: API access for agritech, insurance, lending

## GTM
- FPO partnerships (primary channel)
- KVK collaborations
- NGO field programs
- WhatsApp integration (future)
- State agriculture department pilots

## 90-Day Pilot Proposal
- Phase 1 (Day 1-30): Setup, onboarding 50-100 farmers
- Phase 2 (Day 31-60): Active usage, feedback collection
- Phase 3 (Day 61-90): Impact assessment, scaling decision

## Risks and Mitigations
- **Retention risk:** Focus on recurring use cases (daily decisions, not one-time lookups)
- **Accuracy risk:** RAG with curated sources + uncertainty disclosure + expert escalation
- **Language risk:** Start with Hindi, expand based on pilot feedback
- **Monetization risk:** B2B2C first (partners pay), freemium later
- **Competition risk:** Trust and simplicity as moat, not features
