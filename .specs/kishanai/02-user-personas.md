# 02 - User Personas: KisanAI

## Document Purpose
Define the five primary user personas for KisanAI to guide product decisions, feature prioritization, UX design, and GTM strategy. Each persona is derived from the research document's ICP hypotheses, pain-point evidence, and market analysis.

---

## Persona 1: Ramesh -- Smallholder Farmer

### Demographics
- **Name**: Ramesh (representative name)
- **Age**: 32
- **Location**: Village in Maharashtra (onion/tomato belt)
- **Landholding**: 2.5 acres
- **Education**: 8th standard
- **Language**: Marathi (functional Hindi, no English)
- **Device**: Budget Android smartphone (Rs 8,000-12,000)
- **Internet**: Mobile data (Jio), WhatsApp daily user
- **Income**: Rs 1.5-3 lakh/year (highly variable by season)

### Behavioral Profile
- Checks WhatsApp first thing in the morning
- Relies on local agri-input dealer for pest/disease advice
- Visits mandi 2-3 times per week during harvest season
- Has heard of government schemes but never successfully applied online
- Shares photos of crop problems in family WhatsApp group
- Trusts advice from neighbours and local leaders over apps

### Jobs to Be Done
1. **When** I see unusual spots on my tomato leaves, **I want to** know what disease it is and what to do, **so that** I can act before it spreads and I lose the crop.
2. **When** I'm deciding whether to go to the mandi today, **I want to** know current prices and whether they're trending up or down, **so that** I don't sell at a loss.
3. **When** heavy rains are forecast, **I want to** understand what it means for my specific crop, **so that** I can take protective action.
4. **When** I hear about a new government scheme, **I want to** know if I'm eligible and how to apply, **so that** I don't miss out on subsidies.

### Pain Points
- "I spray what the dealer tells me, but sometimes it doesn't work and I waste money"
- "I can't read the English on most farming apps"
- "I've lost money selling at the wrong time because I didn't know prices were falling"
- "Government websites are too complicated -- I just give up"

### KisanAI Usage Pattern
- Primary channel: WhatsApp
- Frequency: 3-5 queries/week during crop season, 1-2/week off-season
- Trigger moments: disease symptoms, weather alerts, mandi visit planning
- Likely to share answer cards in farmer WhatsApp groups

### Success Criteria for KisanAI
- Ramesh can get a disease diagnosis from a photo in under 2 minutes
- He receives weather advice in Marathi that tells him what to do (not just numbers)
- He checks mandi prices through KisanAI before every mandi visit
- He tells at least 2 other farmers about KisanAI within the first month

### Quote
> "If this app can tell me what's wrong with my crop in Marathi and tell me what to do, I'll use it every day."

## Persona 2: Suresh -- Progressive Farmer

### Demographics
- **Name**: Suresh
- **Age**: 40
- **Location**: District town in Andhra Pradesh (paddy/chilli belt)
- **Landholding**: 8 acres (owns 5, leases 3)
- **Education**: 12th standard + some agricultural training
- **Language**: Telugu (functional Hindi, basic English)
- **Device**: Mid-range Android (Rs 15,000-20,000)
- **Internet**: Broadband at home + mobile data
- **Income**: Rs 5-8 lakh/year

### Behavioral Profile
- Active on YouTube watching agricultural content in Telugu
- Uses Plantix occasionally for disease identification
- Part of 3-4 WhatsApp farming groups
- Has visited KVK for training sessions
- Experimenting with drip irrigation and organic inputs
- Tracks prices on Agmarknet but finds it hard to use
- Helps other farmers in his village with technical questions

### Jobs to Be Done
1. **When** I'm considering a new crop variety or input, **I want to** get evidence-based advice in my language, **so that** I can reduce risk on my leased land.
2. **When** I need to compare prices across multiple mandis, **I want to** see a simple summary with trends, **so that** I can choose the best market.
3. **When** a government scheme deadline is approaching, **I want to** get a reminder with eligibility check, **so that** I don't miss subsidies I qualify for.
4. **When** I help other farmers with their problems, **I want to** quickly verify my advice, **so that** I don't mislead them.

### Pain Points
- "Plantix is useful but sometimes it pushes me to buy chemicals I don't want"
- "I spend too much time checking prices across different mandis"
- "I know about schemes but the application process is a nightmare"
- "I want data-driven advice, not just opinions"

### KisanAI Usage Pattern
- Primary channel: PWA (prefers app-like experience) + WhatsApp for quick checks
- Frequency: 5-10 queries/week
- Uses disease triage, mandi comparison, and scheme eligibility features
- May recommend KisanAI to other farmers in his WhatsApp groups

### Success Criteria for KisanAI
- Suresh gets more accurate disease diagnosis than Plantix for his primary crops
- He can compare mandi prices across 3-4 markets in one query
- He successfully applies for at least one scheme using KisanAI guidance
- He becomes an informal ambassador in his farming community

### Quote
> "I want an app that gives me honest, data-backed advice without trying to sell me something."

## Persona 3: Anjali -- FPO Manager

### Demographics
- **Name**: Anjali
- **Age**: 35
- **Location**: District headquarters in Karnataka
- **Role**: Manager of a Farmer Producer Organization (500+ members)
- **Education**: B.Sc. Agriculture
- **Language**: Kannada (fluent English, Hindi)
- **Device**: Laptop + smartphone
- **Internet**: Broadband + mobile
- **Organization**: FPO with 500-2,000 farmer members across 15 villages

### Behavioral Profile
- Manages FPO operations including aggregation, input procurement, and market linkages
- Coordinates with government agencies for scheme disbursement
- Conducts farmer training sessions and field visits
- Tracks member data in spreadsheets (crop, land, contact)
- Struggles with member engagement and information dissemination
- Uses WhatsApp broadcasts to share information but knows most messages are unread

### Jobs to Be Done
1. **When** I need to disseminate advisory information to all members, **I want to** send localized, actionable advice that farmers actually read, **so that** adoption improves.
2. **When** a farmer member reports a crop problem, **I want to** quickly triage it and provide guidance, **so that** I can serve more farmers with my limited time.
3. **When** a new government scheme is announced, **I want to** quickly assess which members are eligible, **so that** I can help them apply before the deadline.
4. **When** I report to my board or donors, **I want to** show engagement metrics and farmer outcomes, **so that** I can justify funding and demonstrate impact.

### Pain Points
- "I can't personally visit every farm -- I need scalable ways to help"
- "Farmers don't read long messages -- they need short, local-language advice"
- "I spend hours on the phone answering the same questions repeatedly"
- "I can't track which advice actually helped which farmer"

### KisanAI Usage Pattern
- Primary channel: Admin dashboard (web) + WhatsApp for member-facing communication
- Uses bulk advisory features and member analytics
- May white-label or co-brand KisanAI for FPO members
- Needs reporting for donor/board presentations

### Success Criteria for KisanAI
- Anjali can serve 5x more farmer queries without increasing staff
- Member engagement (measured by query volume) increases by 30%+
- She can generate a monthly impact report showing advice given and outcomes
- At least 20% of members actively use KisanAI through the FPO channel

### Quote
> "If I could give every farmer in my FPO a personal agricultural advisor in Kannada, that would transform our impact. KisanAI could be that advisor."

## Persona 4: Priya -- NGO Program Worker

### Demographics
- **Name**: Priya
- **Age**: 28
- **Location**: Field office in Madhya Pradesh (soybean/wheat belt)
- **Role**: Program officer at an agricultural development NGO
- **Education**: MSW or M.Sc. in Agriculture
- **Language**: Hindi (functional English)
- **Device**: Smartphone + occasionally laptop
- **Internet**: Mobile data, patchy in remote areas
- **Program**: Runs climate-resilience program covering 2,000+ farmers

### Behavioral Profile
- Conducts field visits 3-4 days/week
- Organizes farmer training workshops
- Collects baseline and endline data for impact evaluation
- Works with government extension workers (KVK, ATMA)
- Manages program budget and donor reporting
- Needs to demonstrate measurable outcomes for funding renewal

### Jobs to Be Done
1. **When** I'm training farmers on disease management, **I want to** show them a tool they can use after I leave, **so that** the training has lasting impact.
2. **When** I need to collect crop-health data across my program area, **I want to** use farmer-submitted photos and queries as data sources, **so that** I can identify emerging problems early.
3. **When** I report to donors, **I want to** show digital engagement metrics and farmer adoption, **so that** I can justify continued or expanded funding.
4. **When** farmers in my program face a crisis (disease outbreak, extreme weather), **I want to** push urgent advisories instantly, **so that** response time is minimized.

### Pain Points
- "We train farmers but they forget 80% within a month"
- "I can't be everywhere -- I need tools that work when I'm not there"
- "Donors want digital metrics but most of our work is in-person"
- "Early warning for disease outbreaks would save so much crop loss"

### KisanAI Usage Pattern
- Primary channel: WhatsApp (for farmer-facing) + dashboard (for monitoring)
- Frequency: Daily monitoring of program-area queries
- Uses analytics to track farmer engagement and emerging issues
- May integrate KisanAI into training curriculum

### Success Criteria for KisanAI
- Farmers in Priya's program show measurable increase in adoption of recommended practices
- Priya can identify a disease outbreak 3-5 days earlier than traditional methods
- Donor report includes KisanAI engagement data showing sustained farmer usage
- Training workshops include live KisanAI demo and farmer onboarding

### Quote
> "The best training tool is one farmers keep using after the workshop ends. If KisanAI can be that tool, it changes everything for our program."

## Persona 5: Vikram -- Agri-Input Retailer

### Demographics
- **Name**: Vikram
- **Age**: 45
- **Location**: Small town market in Telangana
- **Role**: Owner of agri-input shop (seeds, fertilizers, pesticides)
- **Education**: 10th standard
- **Language**: Telugu (basic Hindi)
- **Device**: Smartphone (used for payments and WhatsApp)
- **Internet**: Mobile data
- **Business**: Serves 200-500 farmer customers, Rs 30-50 lakh annual turnover

### Behavioral Profile
- Recommends products to farmers based on their crop problems
- Relies on company representatives for product knowledge
- Knows most farmers by name and their crop history
- WhatsApp is primary business communication tool
- Attends dealer meets organized by input companies
- Trust is his biggest business asset

### Jobs to Be Done
1. **When** a farmer brings a diseased leaf to my shop, **I want to** quickly identify the problem and recommend the right product, **so that** I maintain trust and make a sale.
2. **When** I want to help my customers make better decisions, **I want to** share useful advisory content, **so that** they see me as a trusted advisor, not just a shopkeeper.
3. **When** a farmer asks about government schemes, **I want to** give them accurate information, **so that** I strengthen my relationship with them.
4. **When** I want to attract new customers, **I want to** offer a valuable free service, **so that** farmers have a reason to visit my shop.

### Pain Points
- "Farmers sometimes blame me when the product I recommended doesn't work"
- "I don't always know the right diagnosis -- I'm not a trained agronomist"
- "If I could offer advisory as a service, farmers would come to me first"
- "Company reps only push their own products -- I need neutral advice"

### KisanAI Usage Pattern
- Primary channel: WhatsApp on his phone (show to walk-in customers)
- Uses KisanAI as a "second opinion" tool when farmers bring problems
- May display KisanAI QR code in his shop for farmer self-service
- Frequency: 5-15 queries/day (serving multiple farmers)

### Success Criteria for KisanAI
- Vikram uses KisanAI to verify at least 50% of his disease recommendations
- His customers report higher satisfaction with his advice
- At least 10 farmers per month discover and try KisanAI through his shop
- He becomes a KisanAI advocate, recommending it to other dealers

### Quote
> "If this tool can tell me what's really wrong with a farmer's crop -- and it's right most of the time -- I'll use it every day. My reputation depends on good advice."

## Design Implications

### For Ramesh (Smallholder)
- Default to Marathi; no English required
- Maximum 2 taps to ask a question
- Voice input as primary (not just text)
- Answer cards designed for WhatsApp sharing
- No registration required

### For Suresh (Progressive)
- PWA with dashboard-like features
- Mandi price comparison across markets
- Detailed disease diagnosis with confidence scores
- Scheme eligibility with application links

### For Anjali (FPO Manager)
- Admin dashboard with member analytics
- Bulk advisory sending capability
- Impact report generation
- Co-branding options

### For Priya (NGO Worker)
- Program-area analytics and early warning
- Integration with training workflows
- Donor-friendly reporting formats
- Offline-capable for field visits

### For Vikram (Retailer)
- Quick-scan disease diagnosis for walk-in customers
- Product-neutral advice (not tied to specific brands)
- QR code for shop display
- Simple interface for showing to customers
