# KisanAI Research Summary

## One-Line Pitch
KisanAI is a vernacular, source-aware AI farming assistant that helps Indian farmers make better daily crop decisions.

## Pain Points
1. Fragmented information — weather, mandi, schemes, disease advice all in different places
2. Language barriers — most tools in English/Hindi, regional languages underserved
3. Complex government schemes — eligibility buried in PDFs and bureaucratic language
4. Disease uncertainty — farmers guess or rely on dealers, wrong treatments waste money

## Market Opportunity
- 9.7 Cr digitally traceable farmers (PM-KISAN)
- 41.7% rural internet penetration
- 5G covers 99% districts
- TAM: Rs 58.2B (@ Rs 600/farmer/year)

## Competition
- Bayer FarmRise: 5M+ users, corporate trust deficit
- DeHaat: Rs 3,000 Cr revenue, commerce-first
- Bharat-VISTAAR: Government backed, limited personalization
- Plantix: Strong disease detection, single-feature

## ICP
- Smallholder farmer with smartphone
- Progressive farmer comfortable with apps
- FPO manager serving farmer groups
- NGO field worker running agriculture programs

## Wedge
Vernacular, messaging-first advisory assistant for recurring daily decisions: disease triage, weather-to-action, mandi context, scheme eligibility.

## GTM
- FPO partnerships (primary)
- KVK collaborations
- NGO field programs
- State agriculture department pilots

## Risks
- Retention: focus on recurring use cases
- Accuracy: RAG + curated sources + uncertainty disclosure
- Language: start Hindi, expand based on feedback
- Monetization: B2B2C first, freemium later
