# KisanAI Pitch Page Content Architecture

## Purpose

The `/pitch` page is a research-backed investor, government, FPO, NGO, and strategic partner microsite. It should not read like a generic marketing page. It should read like a serious founder has converted a data room, pilot proposal, and investor Q&A into a public, defensible page.

Primary goal: build trust with readers who are evaluating whether KisanAI is a credible pilot-ready AI agriculture infrastructure startup.

Secondary goals:
- Explain the agriculture opportunity without inflating addressability.
- Show why KisanAI starts with decision support rather than a broad farm super-app.
- Make assumptions explicit.
- Convert serious readers to a pilot or investor conversation.
- Give the founder a reusable fundraising asset.

## Core Content Rules

- Do not claim traction, revenue, users, pilots, partnerships, certifications, yield improvement, or government endorsement unless directly sourced.
- Use "pilot-ready", "early-stage", "assumption", "pilot hypothesis", and "to be validated" where proof is not yet available.
- Use the deep research report and investor Q&A dossier as the highest-priority source.
- Preserve exact source URLs where available, especially from the deep research report.
- Treat citation markers in the older research files as internal crawl references when raw URLs are absent; do not display those as external citations unless the original URL is known.
- Separate proof from plan. Example: "FarmRise crossed 5 million users" is proof of category adoption; "KisanAI can reach farmers through FPOs" is a distribution hypothesis.
- Use sober investor language. Avoid "revolutionary", "guaranteed", "no competition", "India's first" unless explicitly caveated as existing public positioning rather than a verified claim.

## Section Architecture

### 1. Hero

Required headline:

`KisanAI: AI Decision Infrastructure for Indian Agriculture`

Required subheadline:

`A vernacular-first AI farming assistant helping farmers, FPOs, and agri institutions make faster, safer, source-backed decisions across crop advisory, disease triage, weather action, mandi context, and scheme navigation.`

Content blocks:
- One-line pitch.
- 30-second pitch.
- Trust note: "Pilot-ready, not claiming fake traction."
- CTAs: View Investor Q&A, View Data Proof, Download / Print Pitch, Contact for Pilot.

  - FarmRise 5 million users as category adoption proof.

  - Bharat-VISTAAR as government category validation.

Design: full-width, quiet premium intro with proof chips and compact metric cards. No playful mascot treatment.

| Module | User pain | KisanAI flow | Proof metric |

## Citation Model

Use `PitchSource` and `PitchClaim` types.

Each visible metric should reference one or more source IDs. If a claim lacks a source, use an assumption badge. Sources should open in a new tab.

Source confidence:
- High: official/reputable citation.
- Medium: cited media/secondary source.
- Low: assumption or needs validation.

## Known Content Gaps

Must be labeled as open or to validate:
- Founder credentials and team depth.
- Actual users and retention.
- Revenue and pricing.
- Signed pilots and partnerships.
- Accuracy of disease detection.
- Legal entity and ownership.
- Full privacy/security implementation.
- Real CAC/LTV.
- Government procurement pathway.
